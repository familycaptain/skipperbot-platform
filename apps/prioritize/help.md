# Prioritize

Cut the noise to a few things that matter — up to **3 focus slots** for right
now, backed by a single backlog gathered from across your apps.

## Overview

Prioritize answers "what should I work on?" Each person has at most **3 focus
slots** — the priorities they're actively on. Behind that is a **backlog** that
automatically pulls actionable items from Goals, Reminders, Nags, To-Do, Auto
issues, and other apps into one ranked list, so you decide from everything in one
place instead of hopping between apps. When a focus item is done, you clear it and
promote the next thing.

## Screens

- **Focus.** Your up-to-3 current priorities.
- **Backlog.** Everything actionable, gathered from across your apps and grouped
  by type — your menu for what to pull into focus next.
- **Family focus.** A view of what everyone is focused on.

## Example workflows

**See / set your priorities**
- *In the app:* open Prioritize — focus slots up top, backlog below; drag/pick
  items into focus.
- *Through chat:* "what are my priorities?" (also opens the app), "what should I
  focus on today?" (shows the backlog and suggests).

**Promote / clear focus**
- *Through chat:* "make the kitchen remodel my top priority" (promotes it),
  "I'm done with that one" (clears it, freeing a slot). If all 3 slots are full,
  clear one first.

**Check on others**
- *Through chat:* "what is Alice focused on?", "what's everyone focused on?"

## Tips

- Three slots is the point — it forces real prioritization. Clear something to add something.
- The backlog is read from the other apps live, so it stays current as you work.
- There's a daily nudge to fill empty focus slots; it's toggled in the Prioritize app (not a regular reminder).

## Your data

Your focus selections are **saved and pulled into Skipper's memory**; the backlog
is aggregated live from the apps that own those items (Goals, Reminders, etc.).
Everything stays within your household.
