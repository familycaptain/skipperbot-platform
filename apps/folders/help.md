# Folders

Organize documents and files into named, nestable folders — and because Skipper
indexes what's inside, you can later just *ask* about a folder's contents.

## Overview

Folders group **documents** and **attachments** into collections you can nest
(subfolders), own privately or share, and decorate with icons/colors/tags. The
standout feature is **folder intelligence**: when you add content to a folder,
Skipper extracts facts and builds search embeddings from it, so everything filed
away becomes recallable in chat later — not just findable by name.

## Screens

- **Folder list / tree.** Browse your folders and subfolders with breadcrumbs;
  filter to your own or shared folders.
- **Folder contents.** Open a folder to see the documents and files in it.
- Create folders, create-a-document-directly-in-a-folder, and move/remove items
  between folders.

## Example workflows

**Create a folder and file things**
- *In the app:* create a folder, then add existing docs/files or make a new doc inside it.
- *Through chat:* "make a folder for tax documents", "put that ER write-up in the
  medical folder", "create a 2026 Returns subfolder inside Taxes".

**Find what's filed**
- *In the app:* open the folder, or search folders by name.
- *Through chat:* "what's in the medical folder?", "search folders for tax".

**Recall by meaning (folder intelligence)**
- *Through chat:* because folder contents are indexed, ask things like "what did
  the inspection report say about the roof?" and Skipper can answer from a filed doc.

## Tips

- Folders cut across apps — a folder can hold documents and attachments together.
- Nest folders for structure (Taxes → 2026 Returns), and share or keep private per folder.

## Your data

Folders, their structure, and **the facts/embeddings extracted from their
contents are saved and pulled into Skipper's memory** — that's what makes filed
material recallable in chat later. Everything stays within your household.
