# Home

A home-maintenance hub — track the recurring upkeep your house needs (filters,
gutters, septic, smoke detectors) and what's due, so nothing slips.

## Overview

Home keeps your household maintenance on schedule. Add recurring or one-off
tasks, and Home tracks when each is due, flags what's overdue, and keeps a log of
completions. Due/overdue items also surface in the **Prioritize** app so they
show up alongside the rest of what needs attention. You can group tasks into
categories (Pool, Roofing, Landscaping, …).

## Screens

- **Tasks.** All home tasks with a due indicator — 🔴 overdue, 🟡 due soon,
  🟢 on schedule — and their interval/next-due.
- **Task detail.** Edit name/interval/due, activate/deactivate, and see the
  completion history.
- **Categories.** Configure the categories tasks are grouped under.
- **Maintenance log.** Recent completions across all tasks.

## Example workflows

**Add a recurring task**
- *In the app:* create a task with an interval (30 = monthly, 90 = quarterly, 365
  = yearly) and a next-due date.
- *Through chat:* "add a quarterly task to replace the HVAC filter".

**Mark something done**
- *In the app:* complete the task — recurring ones auto-advance to the next due date.
- *Through chat:* "I changed the A/C filter" → Skipper finds the matching task and
  completes it (advancing its next due), rather than creating a duplicate. Add a
  note if useful ("used a MERV-13").

**See what's due**
- *In the app:* scan the colored due indicators.
- *Through chat:* "what home maintenance is due this month?", "what's overdue
  around the house?"

## Tips

- Recurring tasks auto-reset their next due date on completion; one-off tasks just complete.
- Due items also appear in **Prioritize**; reminders/nudges lean on **Reminders/Schedules**.
- Mention a completion in plain language — Skipper matches it to an existing task instead of duplicating.

## Your data

Your home tasks, categories, and completion log are **saved in the database and
pulled into Skipper's memory**, so you can ask "when did we last do the gutters?"
and Skipper knows. It stays within your household.
