# Goals

Plan and track everything from big ambitions to today's to-dos — Goals →
Projects → Tasks — and optionally let Skipper's Project Manager help drive them.

## Overview

Goals is the platform's project-management core. You break an outcome into a
**Goal**, the chunks of work into **Projects**, and the concrete steps into
**Tasks** (which can have subtasks, to any depth). Items carry owners, due dates,
priority, ordering, and free-form **notes** (markdown). Other apps link to
goals/projects/tasks for context. If you assign work to **Skipper**, its
Project-Manager (PM) thinking domain starts proactively nudging it forward.

## Screens

- **Goals overview.** Your goals, each expandable into its projects and tasks.
- **Goal / Project / Task detail.** Structured fields plus a **notes** area
  (markdown narrative — there's no separate description field), child items, due
  dates, owners, dependencies, and attachments.
- **Task tree.** Tasks and subtasks nested under a project.

## Example workflows

**Start a goal**
- *In the app:* create a goal, add projects under it, add tasks under those.
- *Through chat:* "start a goal to plan our summer trip" (Skipper sets up the
  goal and can scaffold projects/tasks).

**Work the list**
- *In the app:* check off tasks, set due dates, reorder, assign owners.
- *Through chat:* "what tasks are due this week?", "mark the passports task done",
  "add a task to book the rental car".

**Let Skipper drive it**
- *Through chat:* "have Skipper own getting the garage organized" → the PM domain
  tracks it and nudges at its normal cadence (gentle, respects quiet hours).

## Tips

- Narrative/detail lives in **notes** (markdown), not a description field.
- Due dates can auto-create reminders; dependencies and ordering control sequence.
- Anything owned/assigned to **Skipper** gets proactive PM follow-up — the
  onboarding goal on a fresh install works exactly this way.

## Your data

Your goals, projects, tasks, notes, and their history are **saved in the database
and pulled into Skipper's memory** — so you can ask "where are we on the trip
goal?" and Skipper knows. It stays within your household.
