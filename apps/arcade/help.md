# Arcade

A small collection of built-in games with a shared high-score board — a quick
break without leaving Skipper.

## Overview

Arcade bundles four original, self-contained games that run right in the
desktop. Three are action games that post to a shared leaderboard; the fourth is
Solitaire, which saves your game so you can come back to it. No setup, no
accounts.

## Screens

- **Game menu.** Cards for each game — pick one to play. Below the cards is the
  **leaderboard** (top runs across the action games).
- **In-game.** Each game runs full-panel with its own controls; a back button
  returns you to the menu, and there's a sound on/off toggle.
- **The games:**
  - **Wardenfall** — top-down tower defense; place defenders and hold the line.
  - **Aeldrift** — relaxed 3D world exploration (WebGL), no enemies.
  - **Spinhazard** — old-school vector asteroids-style shooter.
  - **Solitaire** — classic Klondike (draw-3): click the stock to deal, click a
    card to pick it up, click where it goes, double-click to send a card to a
    foundation. Your game **auto-saves** so you can leave and resume.

## Example workflows

**Play a game**
- *In the app:* open Arcade → click a game.
- *Through chat:* "open the arcade".

**Resume a Solitaire game**
- *In the app:* open Arcade → Solitaire — it restores your in-progress game
  automatically. Use **New game** to start fresh.

**Check the leaderboard**
- *In the app:* the top runs are listed under the game menu.
- *Through chat:* "what are the arcade high scores?"

## Tips

- The three action games share one leaderboard; finishing a run records your score.
- Solitaire saves per person, so each member resumes their own game.

## Your data

Your **high scores** and your **in-progress Solitaire game** are saved as records
**and pulled into Skipper's memory**, so you can ask things like "what's the top
score on Spinhazard?" The games themselves need no other data.
