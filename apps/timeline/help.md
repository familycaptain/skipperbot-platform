# Timeline

A family journal and activity feed — post updates and photos, and automatically
see everything that's happened across Skipper, in one scroll.

## Overview

Timeline is two things at once: a **microblog/journal** where you write posts
(with photos and tags), and an **automatic activity log** — whenever any app
records a change, a timeline entry is created too. So it's both "share what's
going on" and "what changed in our Skipper lately," in a single feed.

## Screens

- **Feed.** Posts newest-first, mixing your journal entries and auto-logged
  activity from other apps; pinned posts stay on top.
- **Post.** A single entry with its full markdown body and any photos; links back
  to the source app when it was auto-generated.
- **Search / tags.** Full-text search and a tag index to find past entries.

## Example workflows

**Write a post**
- *In the app:* create a post (title, body, photos, tags).
- *Through chat:* "post to the timeline: Alice scored her first goal today", "add
  these photos to that post".

**Browse history**
- *In the app:* scroll the feed; filter by tag or author; pin important posts.
- *Through chat:* "what happened today?", "show recent activity", "find timeline
  posts tagged vacation".

## Tips

- Most apps emit a timeline entry automatically, so it doubles as an at-a-glance
  audit trail of recent changes.
- Tags are comma-separated and auto-lowercased; pinned posts stick to the top.

## Your data

Your posts, photos, and tags — and the auto-logged activity entries — are **saved
in the database and pulled into Skipper's memory**, so "what happened last week?"
works. Everything stays within your household.
