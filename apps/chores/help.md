# Chores

Recurring household chores that rotate across the kids automatically, with a
daily reminder and easy check-off.

## Overview

Chores handles the **recurring, unpaid** rotation — who has which chore on which
day — so nobody has to track it on a whiteboard. Chores are grouped into
**zones** (e.g. a bathroom, a bedroom) and rotate among the kids assigned to that
zone. Each morning every kid gets a push with their chores for the day, and they
check them off as they go. (Paid one-off tasks live in the **Bounties** app, not
here.)

## Screens

- **Today.** Each kid's chores for the day, with check-off.
- **Week view.** The upcoming rotation across the week.
- **Manage (parents).** Add/edit **kids**, **zones**, and **chores** (which days a
  chore runs, which kids rotate through a zone).

## Example workflows

**See today's chores**
- *In the app:* open Chores → Today.
- *Through chat:* "what chores does Alice have today?", "what are my chores?"

**Check one off**
- *In the app:* tap the chore to mark it done.
- *Through chat:* "I took out the trash", "mark the bathroom done".

**Set things up (parent)**
- *In the app:* add a zone, add the kids to it, add chores and the days they run.
- *Through chat:* "add a Friday chore to vacuum the living room".

## Tips

- Kids can check off their own chores; **parents** manage kids/zones/chores.
- Chores pairs with **Reminders/Notifications** so the right kid gets the morning nudge.
- For tasks that pay out, use **Bounties** instead.

## Your data

Your kids, zones, chores, and completions are **saved in the database and pulled
into Skipper's memory**, so you can ask "did Alice do her chores today?" and
Skipper knows. It stays within your household.

> Note: the rotation/scheduling is currently modeled on our household's setup;
> making it fully configurable for any family is tracked separately.
