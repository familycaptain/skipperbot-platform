# Behaviors

Teach Skipper your own "if this, then that" rules — things it should *always* do
when you say a certain kind of thing.

## Overview

A behavior is a standing rule: a **trigger** ("when I say I started my truck")
and an **action** ("mark the matching auto-maintenance item done"). Unlike
memories (which Skipper recalls only when relevant), behaviors are **always
active** — they're injected into every conversation for that person, so
automation-style rules fire reliably. You usually create them just by telling
Skipper, in plain language, and they're managed per person.

## Screens

- **Your rules.** A list of your active behaviors — each showing its trigger and
  action — where you can review, edit, or remove them.
- Most rules are created from chat (below) rather than typed in here, but the app
  is where you see and prune them.

## Example workflows

**Create a rule**
- *Through chat:* say a "from now on" / "whenever" instruction, e.g.
  - "From now on, when I say I finished a chore, check my to-do and mark the match done."
  - "Whenever I mention exercising, remind me to log my workout."
  - "If I say I started my truck, mark that auto-maintenance item complete."
  Skipper recognizes the teaching moment and saves it as a behavior.

**See or change your rules**
- *In the app:* open Behaviors to review the list; edit or delete a rule.
- *Through chat:* "what behavior rules do I have?", "stop the workout reminder rule".

## Tips

- Use **behaviors** for *action* rules that must always fire ("when I say X, do
  Y"). Use **memories** for facts/preferences ("remember I prefer oat milk").
- Rules are per person — yours don't affect anyone else's chats.
- Keep triggers specific so they fire on the right phrases.

## Your data

Your behavior rules are **saved in the database and injected into your chats**
(and visible to Skipper's memory), so they're always in force and you can ask
"what rules do I have?" They're per-user and stay within your household.
