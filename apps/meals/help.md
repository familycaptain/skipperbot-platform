# Meals

Decide what to eat and remember what you've had — a filterable library of meal
ideas plus a log of what was actually eaten.

## Overview

Meals helps answer "what's for dinner?" It keeps two things: a **library of meal
ideas** (dishes you make, tagged by cuisine, occasion, and character/effort) and a
**meal log** (what was actually eaten, when). Filter the library by cuisine,
effort, or tags to decide; log a meal when you eat it (which also grows the
library automatically). It pairs with **Recipes** (the how-to) and **Lists**
(the shopping).

## Screens

- **Meal library.** Browse/filter your meal ideas by cuisine, effort, occasion,
  and tags — your "what could we make?" menu.
- **Meal log.** A history of what was eaten, by date and meal type
  (breakfast/lunch/dinner/snack).
- **Decide.** Filter the library down to land on a choice for tonight.

## Example workflows

**Decide what to eat**
- *In the app:* filter the library (e.g. Italian + low effort) and pick.
- *Through chat:* "what's for dinner tonight?", "give me 3 quick Mexican dinner
  ideas", "what can I make that's vegetarian?"

**Add a meal idea (library only)**
- *Through chat:* "add chicken parmesan to my meal ideas" — adds it to the library,
  no log entry.

**Log what you ate (grows the library too)**
- *Through chat:* "we had grilled chicken for dinner" — records the event and
  finds-or-creates the meal idea.

**Look back**
- *Through chat:* "what did we eat last week?", "how often do we have pasta?"

## Tips

- "Meal idea" = library only (not eaten); naming a meal type inside an idea
  ("a dinner idea is…") describes when it suits, not that you ate it.
- Tags are the classification — cuisine + occasion + a descriptor (method/diet).
- Use **Recipes** for the actual instructions and **Lists** for the grocery run.

## Your data

Your meal ideas and meal log are **saved in the database and pulled into Skipper's
memory**, so you can ask "what have we been eating?" or "what's a good dinner
idea?" any time. It stays within your household.
